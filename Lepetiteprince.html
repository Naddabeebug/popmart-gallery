The Fox

The Presence of Absence

With quiet patience, the Fox teaches that true connections take time. It symbolizes the deep longing for companionship and the pain that comes with love, but also its irreplaceable value—because “you become responsible forever for what you have tamed.”

The Rose

Beautiful yet thorned, the Rose stands as a symbol of love’s complexity—delicate, demanding, and sometimes misunderstood. It reminds us that love is not about perfection but about choosing to care, even when it is difficult.

The Businessman

The Cost of Endless Pursuit

Obsessed with counting his wealth, this figure represents the relentless chase for material success. It challenges us to ask: What are we really working for? And at what cost?

Geographer

The Distance Between Knowing and Living

Surrounded by maps but never traveling, the Geographer reminds us that knowledge without experience is incomplete. It is a call to step beyond the familiar and embrace the unknown.

The Switch Man

The Road We Choose

Observing trains that never stop, this figure reflects on the rush of life and the choices that define our path. It reminds us to pause and ask: Are we moving toward what truly matters?

The Snake

The Inevitability of Change

Coiled and watchful, the Snake symbolizes the transformative power of endings. It reminds us that letting go is not just loss but also renewal, an essential part of life’s journey.

The King

The Illusion of Control

Dressed in regal robes but with no one to rule, this figure reflects the emptiness of authority without understanding. It reminds us that true leadership is not about control but about connection and wisdom.

The Conceited Man

Always seeking applause, this figure mirrors the part of us that craves recognition. It reminds us that self-worth cannot be measured by others’ approval and that a life built on vanity is ultimately hollow.
